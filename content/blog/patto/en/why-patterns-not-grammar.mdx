## You know the grammar. So why can't you speak?

You've studied English for years. You know *when to use "the"*, you understand the *past perfect*, you can explain the difference between *"since" and "for"*.

But when someone speaks to you in English — your mind goes blank.

This is the **grammar trap**. It's where most English learners get stuck.

---

## How did native speakers actually learn?

Native speakers didn't start with grammar rules. They absorbed **patterns** — natural chunks of language that repeat across hundreds of situations.

When a native speaker says *"I was wondering if you could..."*, they're not thinking:

> *"I need to use past progressive plus a conditional clause to soften this request..."*

They just know. It comes out automatically. It's muscle memory.

That's the difference between *knowing about* English and *being able to use* English.

## Why Patto is different

Patto is built on one core principle:

> **Patterns become automatic when they're repeated in context.**

Here's how it works:

**1. Stories, not sentences**
Every pattern is embedded inside a real story. Your brain is designed to remember patterns alongside meaning, emotion, and context — not as isolated rules to memorize.

**2. A trainer guides you**
Patto's trainer controls the pace of your learning. You don't have to decide when to move forward — the trainer does. Your job is to listen, respond, and speak. No decision fatigue, just flow.

**3. Spaced repetition is built in**
Each pattern reappears across multiple stories, spaced over time. By the time you finish a pattern set, you'll have encountered it enough times to remember it naturally — without feeling like you're "studying."

**4. Active recall, not passive review**
Patto hides the pattern and makes you retrieve it. This is the most effective way to move language from short-term recognition to long-term use.

Patto's goal isn't to teach you *about* English.

It's to make English patterns feel as natural as your native language — to the point where the words come before the thought.
